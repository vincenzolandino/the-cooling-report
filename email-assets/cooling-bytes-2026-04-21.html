Technology
Eaton Says AI Data Centers Need Aerospace-Grade Engineering. At 600 kW Per Rack, the Argument Is Hard to Dismiss.
Peter de Bock, VP of Data Center Energy and Cooling at Eaton, argues that NVIDIA Vera Rubin racks at 600 kW produce thermal density comparable to an internal combustion engine running continuously. PUE masks real efficiency by 0.3 points or more. His proposed replacement: tokens per watt. Hot-water cooling at 45°C in and 60°C out exploits a cubic heat rejection relationship that drops fan power by a factor of eight. The engineering culture has to match the thermal loads.

Google's Data Center Steam Is Grounding Flights in The Dalles, Oregon. The Cooling Industry Has an Externalities Problem.
Cooling towers at Google's $2.4 billion campus in The Dalles produce steam plumes that settle into the Columbia River corridor, forming fog thick enough to divert flights from the nearby airport. Google consumed 550 million gallons of water in a single year for evaporative cooling. Roughly 40% of the city's total. Pilots call the phenomenon the "Google Cloud." The cooling industry needs to reckon with the externalities of open-loop evaporative systems at hyperscale.

Water
EPA Launches WRAP 2.0 to Push Recycled Water Into Data Center Cooling. The Federal Government Just Picked a Side in the Water Fight.
EPA Administrator Lee Zeldin launched the Water Reuse Action Plan 2.0, naming data center cooling as an explicit federal priority. Microsoft's $31 million reuse facility in Quincy, WA reclaims 138 million gallons per year through a 30-mile piping network. Google's Georgia campus already runs on recycled municipal wastewater. WRAP 2.0 is voluntary, but the 200-partner coalition signals consensus. Recycled water becomes the default cooling supply within five years.

Chinese Researchers Built a Diamond-Copper Composite That Nearly Doubles Cooling Efficiency. The TIM Supply Chain Just Got a New Front.
A team at the Chinese Academy of Sciences deployed a diamond-copper composite thermal interface material at an AI computing node in Zhengzhou, achieving 80% cooling efficiency improvement with thermal conductivity exceeding 1,000 W/mK. The composite also delivered a 10% chip performance gain. China is building domestic TIM production to reduce dependence on Honeywell, Shin-Etsu, and Dow. Expect domestically produced high-conductivity TIMs in Chinese AI servers within three years.

Schneider Electric Maps the Full Liquid Cooling Stack From 50kW to 1MW Per Rack. The Transition Is Already Underway.
Sujit Kamble of Schneider Electric laid out the three-tier cooling progression at a technical session in India. Air tops out at 50 kW per rack. Direct-to-chip removes 80% of heat at source using cold plates. Two-phase immersion supports up to 1 MW. CDU specs include 25-micron filtration, 2.5 MW capacity, and a 20-year expected lifespan. Individual servers now exceed $1 million in value. Cooling reliability is an asset protection problem.

Efficiency
Data Centers Burned 683 TWh in 2024 and Cooling Ate Half of It. Most Operators Still Cannot Measure Where the Energy Goes.
Global data center power consumption hit 683 TWh in 2024. Cooling accounts for up to 50% of the total draw. The IEA projects 1,479 TWh by 2030. Every degree Celsius raised at server inlet saves approximately 4% in cooling energy. Most facilities still overcool because they lack the instrumentation to know what happens at the rack level when setpoints change. PUE is a summary statistic. It does not tell you where the watts go.
