Water
A Georgia QTS Campus Drew 29 Million Gallons of Water Through Unbilled Meters. Residents Found Out When Their Pressure Dropped.
The Annelise Park subdivision noticed low water pressure. The county investigated. Two industrial water connections were feeding the Blackstone-owned QTS campus. One was not on the utility's records. The other was not billed. Total missing draw: 29 million gallons over a period the county and QTS still cannot agree on. Retroactive bill: $147,474. Fine: zero. Fayetteville had already banned new data centers in every zoning district earlier this year. The audit pattern is going to repeat in every county hosting hyperscale construction.

The thermal architecture choice will decide the vote. Closed-loop dry cooling, hybrid systems, or open-loop evaporative towers each carry different odds of clearing the political bar. Microsoft's $1 Billion Kenya Data Center Stalled Over Capacity Payment Guarantees. Geothermal Cooling Was Supposed to Be the Differentiator.
