Welcome to the first Monday in April. Iran struck data center infrastructure in the Gulf this week. The IRGC claimed Oracle's Dubai facility. Bahrain confirmed a fire at Amazon's cloud site. Meanwhile, tariffs are reshaping who can deliver cooling hardware and at what price, Durbin wants your water data reported to the state, and the crypto miners who pivoted to AI owe $48.5 billion worth of facilities they have not built yet. The economics of the AI buildout looked fragile before the missiles. They look more fragile now.

INFRASTRUCTURE
Iran Hit an Oracle Data Center in Dubai and an Amazon Facility in Bahrain. Physical Risk Just Entered the Cooling Conversation.
The IRGC claimed strikes on Oracle's Dubai data center and Amazon's cloud facility in Bahrain. Dubai denied it. Bahrain confirmed a fire at the Amazon site. Outdoor heat rejection equipment, CDU piping, cooling towers: all exposed, none blast-hardened. A facility that loses its cooling loop loses compute in minutes regardless of whether IT equipment took a direct hit. Replacement CDUs are on 16-24 week lead times under normal conditions. The Gulf-region hyperscale buildout priced in many risks. This was not one of them. [Jerusalem Post]
ENERGY
$400 Billion in AI Capex Against $60 Billion in Revenue. Higher Energy Costs Make That Ratio Worse.
The Guardian reports the AI sector spent roughly $400 billion in capital expenditure last year on $60 billion in revenue. A 6.7-to-1 capex-to-revenue ratio financed through complex debt structures including off-balance-sheet SPVs and asset-backed securities. Roughly $120 billion in data center debt was moved off-balance-sheet in the past two years. The Bank of England flagged AI company share prices as vulnerable to energy cost increases before the Iran conflict. The WTO's chief economist said a prolonged period of high energy prices could "crimp" investment. Egypt is imposing shop curfews. Indonesia mandated work-from-home Fridays. The Philippines declared a national energy emergency. Energy is the single largest variable cost for data centers. [The Guardian]
INFRASTRUCTURE
Crypto Miners Worth $48.5 Billion Are Building AI Data Centers. 2026 Is When They Have to Deliver.
Eleven former crypto mining companies have grown from a combined $2.1 billion market cap in late 2022 to $48.5 billion today. Applied Digital: 5 GW of active and planned capacity. Cipher Digital: 4.1 GW. TeraWulf's Lake Mariner project carries a $3.2 billion Google rent guarantee with a walk-away clause if the facility slips 180 days. CoreWeave's CEO said the building boom is "just overwhelming the supply chains." The difference between crypto mining and AI compute: AI racks need vast cooling systems to handle waste heat and have far smaller tolerances for outages. These companies built their valuations on power contracts. Whether they can build cooling infrastructure at scale is the 2026 question. [Business Insider]

SITING
Alaska Wants Data Centers. It Has Cold Air, Cheap Land, and Zero Policy.
The Anchorage Daily News editorial board argues Alaska should be a data center destination: cold climate for free cooling, underutilized grid capacity, available land. The problem is that Alaska has passed zero data center or AI legislation while 38 states enacted roughly 100 AI laws and 21 states passed 40-plus data center measures covering energy, water, zoning, and infrastructure in 2025 alone. Mat-Su Borough is moving forward with a data center partnership. Anchorage is moving in the opposite direction, imposing regulations on an industry that has not arrived yet. Cold air is an asset. Policy vacuum is not. [Anchorage Daily News]
DEMAND
AI Chatbot Traffic Is Growing 7x Faster Than Social Media. It Still Trails by 4x.
Similarweb data: social media pulls 4x more total traffic than AI chatbot services, but AI chatbot traffic is growing 7x faster. 72% of AI tool traffic comes from desktop, compared to roughly 50/50 for social media. That is a work and productivity usage pattern. Enterprise inference workloads running on GPU clusters in liquid-cooled racks. Every chatbot query is a GPU cycle. Every GPU cycle is a watt. Every watt is a thermal load on the cooling system that serves it. The gap between current AI traffic and social media traffic is the capacity that has not been built yet. [The Decoder]
