Schneider Published the Liquid Cooling Deployment Playbook. Most Operators Still Don't Have One.
The liquid cooling market is growing at 30%+ annually. Schneider Electric, through its Motivair acquisition, is pushing direct-to-chip as the reference architecture for AI workloads and just introduced the MCDU-70, a 2.5 MW coolant distribution unit scalable beyond 10 MW. The real substance here is operational: constant differential pressure control to prevent GPU overheating, redundant pumps with UPS on every CDU, and early-stage collaboration between IT vendors and cooling specialists. Most facilities teams are commissioning liquid loops for the first time. Schneider is trying to write the rulebook before someone learns these lessons the expensive way.

Community
Air-Cooled Data Centers Hit 80 Decibels. Communities Are Starting to Push Back.
The cooling method determines the noise profile. Air-cooled facilities produce 60-80 decibels, comparable to a gas lawnmower running continuously. Water-cooled facilities sit at 40-60 decibels, closer to a refrigerator hum. In regions without access to freshwater, air cooling is the only option, and neighbors are measuring the difference. Property values, energy costs, and light pollution are already on the list of community grievances. Noise is joining them. For operators in water-scarce regions who cannot switch to liquid cooling, this creates a regulatory exposure that facility planners rarely model. Cooling method selection is becoming a community relations decision as much as a thermal engineering one.
