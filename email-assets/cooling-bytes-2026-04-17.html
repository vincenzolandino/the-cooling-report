Markets
Data Center Power Hits 1,050 TWh This Year. Cooling Is the Constraint That Decides What Gets Built.
Global data center power consumption crosses 1,050 TWh by end of 2026, roughly the annual electricity use of Japan. Traditional CRAC layouts top out around 40 kW per rack. AI clusters are provisioning at 100 kW and climbing. Direct-to-chip, immersion, and two-phase cut cooling-related power 50-60%. That is the difference between a 200 MW build fitting inside its utility allocation and one that does not. Cooling method is now a capacity-planning decision.

Efficiency
Absorption Chillers Use 25 kW to Deliver 2 MW of Cooling. Onsite Generation Is Now a Cooling Play.
A 2 MW absorption chiller consumes 20-25 kW of electricity. A mechanical chiller at the same capacity draws 500 kW or more. The 90% electrical load reduction rewrites the facility power plan for any campus building around onsite generation. Gas turbines and fuel cells discharge 50-65% of fuel energy as heat. Absorption cooling turns that stream into a capacity play instead of a loss line. By 2030, roughly a third of new data centers may operate as fully onsite-powered.

DataCool Launched Three Air-Handler Lines at 100,000 CFM and 300 Tons. The Question Is Where Air Still Fits.
DataCool, the JohnsonMarCraft division of Arizon Companies, launched Alpine, Glacier, and Kodiak on April 16. Scaling from 2,000 to 100,000 CFM and up to 300 tons, with ECM fans and MERV 8-16 filtration. VP Matt Polizzi frames it around AI demand. The truer read: these units are aimed at the hybrid facility, where direct-to-chip handles the GPU load and AHUs cover storage and network tiers. Over 70% of global capacity still sits in air-cooled buildings that need replacement units as CRACs hit end of life.
