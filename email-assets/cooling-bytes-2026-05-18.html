09  ·  Research  ·  IEEE ITherm, May 2026  ·  Penn State

A reinforcement-learning controller cut cooling energy 25% with no new hardware.

The stranded savings were always in the setpoints. Operators run wide thermal margins because a controls mistake risks a thermal event and a hardware refresh does not pay back fast enough to justify the disruption. A physics-informed agent trained on a digital twin learns the aggressive policy in simulation, then runs it safely. Cooling is roughly 40% of facility electricity, so a quarter of that is about 10% of the whole building, and it is already headed to a commercial deployment through Glacian Technologies.
