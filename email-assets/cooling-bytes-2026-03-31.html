Good morning. March 2026 was the biggest month in data center cooling M&A history. Three legacy industrials spent north of $14 billion buying liquid cooling companies. A UC Riverside study put a $58 billion price tag on the water infrastructure the industry needs. And a 141-year-old Swedish heat exchanger company decided data centers are worth chasing. Here is what you need to know.

MARKET
Ecolab Says Cooling Demand Will Outlast Any AI Bubble. They Just Bet $4.75 Billion on It.
"If there's an AI bubble or not, I don't think it matters." That is Josh Magnuson, Ecolab's general manager for global water solutions, talking to Bloomberg four days after his company agreed to pay $4.75 billion for CoolIT Systems. He projects 20%+ annual growth "for the foreseeable future." KKR bought CoolIT for $270M in 2023. They are exiting at $4.75B. A 15x return in three years. Meanwhile, Ecolab is rolling out a 10-14% energy surcharge on every product and service globally starting April 1. The cooling industry's investment thesis just decoupled from the AI hype cycle entirely.

M&A
Trane Completes LiquidStack Acquisition. Three Deals, $14B+, One Month.
Trane closed its LiquidStack deal on March 3. CEO Dave Regnery made three acquisitions in four months: Stellar Energy Digital for direct-to-chip cold plates, then LiquidStack for two-phase immersion cooling. Combined with Eaton buying Boyd Thermal for $9.5B and Ecolab buying CoolIT for $4.75B, the three largest independent liquid cooling companies all got absorbed by legacy industrials in a single month. Over $14 billion in deal value. LiquidStack CEO Joe Capes stays on. The company claims partial PUE as low as 1.02 with its immersion tanks. The independent liquid cooling era is over. What comes next is integration risk.

Blackstone Buys the Company That Cools Fighter Jets. Data Centers Are Next.
Blackstone Energy Transition Partners took a majority stake in Advanced Cooling Technologies, a Lancaster, PA manufacturer founded by Jon Zuo and Scott Garner in 2003. ACT builds two-phase liquid cooling, heat pipes, phase change materials, and cold plates. Their pumped two-phase systems remove over 850 watts with under 1 watt of pumping power. The company's real moat is defense and aerospace thermal engineering that pure-play DC cooling startups cannot replicate on short timelines. BETP IV closed at its $5.6B hard cap. The portfolio now includes Lancium (grid access), Potomac Energy Center (774MW gas plant in Virginia), and Trystar (backup power). Blackstone is assembling the full AI infrastructure stack. Cooling was the missing layer.

SUSTAINABILITY
The Water Bill for AI: $10B to $58B in New Infrastructure
UC Riverside professor Shaolei Ren and a Caltech team published the most detailed study yet on data center water infrastructure costs. The finding: peak daily demand from evaporative cooling can spike 6 to 30 times above average usage. Without new efficiencies, U.S. data centers could need 697 million to 1.45 billion gallons of additional peak capacity per day by 2030. That rivals New York City's entire daily water supply. Google already consumes 33% of The Dalles, Oregon's water. The city needs $260M in upgrades over 20 years. Google covers about a quarter. The rest falls on 15,000 residents. "Money can't buy more snowpack," Ren said. He is right.

TECHNOLOGY
A 141-Year-Old Heat Exchanger Company Just Entered the Data Center Cooling Market.
Alfa Laval launched FreeWaterLoop at Data Center World in London on March 4. This is their first purpose-built data center product. The $6.6 billion Swedish company has been building plate heat exchangers since 1938. Their corrugated plate design delivers 3 to 5x the heat transfer coefficients of shell-and-tube systems in one-fifth the physical volume. FreeWaterLoop pulls heat from the facility water loop into a natural water source (lake, river, seawater) and returns it. Near-zero net consumption. No evaporation. Pump engineering comes from their subsidiary Framo, which builds the submerged cargo pumps on nearly every liquid-cargo vessel afloat. When a company this old and this large enters your market from the industrial side, the opportunity has crossed a threshold.

SUPPLY CHAIN
Munters Books $200M in Orders and Opens a Factory in Virginia
Munters recorded 2.1 billion SEK ($200M+) in data center cooling orders in 2025. The headline deal: $82 million in Geoclima Circlemiser chillers for a large AI facility, followed by a $91M order from a second colocation operator. The Circlemiser's cylindrical microchannel condenser increases heat exchange surface by 45% in the same footprint as a conventional unit. EER gains hit 15%. Munters is also opening a 200,000 sq ft factory in Daleville, Virginia (Q2 2026), putting chiller production within driving distance of Northern Virginia's hyperscale buildout. Nearly 700,000 sq ft of US manufacturing capacity dedicated to data center cooling. The bet: proximity and integration win the supply chain race.
