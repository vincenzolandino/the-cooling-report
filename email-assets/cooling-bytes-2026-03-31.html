Good morning. March 2026 was the biggest month in data center cooling M&A history. Three legacy industrials spent north of $14 billion buying liquid cooling companies. A UC Riverside study put a $58 billion price tag on the water infrastructure the industry needs. And a 141-year-old Swedish heat exchanger company decided data centers are worth chasing. Here is what you need to know.

MARKET
Ecolab Says Cooling Demand Will Outlast Any AI Bubble. They Just Bet $4.75 Billion on It.
Josh Magnuson told Bloomberg that cooling demand will grow 20%+ per year regardless of whether AI is a bubble. KKR is exiting CoolIT with a 15x return in three years. Ecolab is also slapping a 10-14% energy surcharge on all products starting April 1. The cooling industry's investment case just decoupled from the hype cycle.

Trane Completes LiquidStack Acquisition. Three Deals, $14B+, One Month.
Trane closed its LiquidStack deal on March 3, adding two-phase immersion cooling to its stack. Combined with Eaton/Boyd ($9.5B) and Ecolab/CoolIT ($4.75B), three HVAC incumbents absorbed the three largest independent liquid cooling companies in a single month. CEO Dave Regnery now owns chillers, cold plates, and immersion tanks under one roof.

SUSTAINABILITY
The Water Bill for AI: $10B to $58B in New Infrastructure
UC Riverside professor Shaolei Ren and a Caltech team found that data center peak water demand could require up to 1.45 billion gallons of additional daily capacity by 2030. That rivals NYC's entire water supply. Peak demand spikes 6-30x above average. Google now consumes 33% of The Dalles, Oregon's water. The study argues the industry has been measuring the wrong thing.

TECHNOLOGY
Alfa Laval Launches FreeWaterLoop. The Industrial Giants Are Coming.
The $6.6B Swedish heat exchanger company launched its first purpose-built data center product at Data Center World in London. FreeWaterLoop combines 88 years of plate heat exchanger R&D with Framo pump engineering. It pulls heat from the facility loop into a natural water source and returns it with near-zero net consumption. When a company founded in 1883 enters your market, the opportunity is real.

Record 2.1 billion SEK in data center cooling orders in 2025, anchored by an $82M Geoclima Circlemiser chiller deal for an AI facility.
